Alvin Harper | Career and Profession 

Before jumping into Harper’s professional career, let’s look into his successful college career. 

College Career

Alvin accepted a football scholarship from the University of Tennessee.

As a freshman, he appeared in 12 games as a backup wide receiver, making 15 receptions for 247 yards and one touchdown.

He had a 45-yard touchdown reception in the second game against Colorado State University.

Moreover, as a sophomore, he became the team’s starting wide receiver and appeared in 11 games, posting 37 receptions for 487 yards and 5 touchdowns.

In his first start in the fifth game against Washington State University, he set a school single-game record with 12 receptions.

Furthermore, as a junior, he appeared in 11 games, tallying 13 receptions for 246 yards and 2 touchdowns.

Likewise, as a senior, Alvin earned All-Southeastern Conference Freshman First Team honors in 1987 and All-Southeastern Conference first-team honors in 1990.

He was also the MVP of the 1991 Hula Bowl and finished his NCAA football career with 103 receptions for 1,547 yards and 16 touchdowns.

Similarly, Alvin continued to excel in track and field, capturing the 1989 Southeastern Conference indoor high jump championship with a jump of 7 ft 2½in.

He also anchored the University of Tennessee 4×100 and 4×400 meters relays.

Professional Career

Dallas Cowboys

Furthermore, Harper was selected by the Dallas Cowboys in the first round (12th overall) of the 1991 NFL Draft to find a receiving partner for starter Michael Irvin.

He caught 20 passes for 326 yards and one touchdown as a rookie, helping Dallas finish 11–5, its first winning season since 1985.

He missed the seventh game against the Cincinnati Bengals because of a neck injury.

Moreover, in 1992, Alvin rushed once for 15 yards and caught 35 passes for 562 yards and four touchdowns.

He probably had the signature play of his career during the NFC Championship against the San Francisco 49ers, catching a slant route for 70 yards, setting up the Cowboys on the 10-yard line, and sealing the win.

Tampa Bay Buccaneers

On March 8, 1995, Harper signed a lucrative free-agent contract with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers based on his big-play ability and championship experience. 

Though he had a career-high 46 receptions for 633 yards and 2 touchdowns in his first season in Tampa, he could not live up to his free-agent billing.

Alvin missed the first 3 games of the season because of a left sprained medial collateral and a sprained left ankle in the first preseason game against the New York Jets.

Moreover, Harper clinched a 14-6 win in the fourth game against the Washington Redskins with a touchdown reception in the final 6 minutes.

His best game came against the Cincinnati Bengals with 6 receptions for 117 yards and no touchdowns.

Likewise, in 1996, Alvin appeared in 12 games with 7 starts, registering 19 receptions for 289 yards and one touchdown.

His top receiving game came in the third game against the Denver Broncos, making 2 receptions for 77 yards and one touchdown.

Similarly, Harper had 4 receptions for 41 yards and no touchdowns in the fifth game against the Detroit Lions.

Harper was replaced with Robb Thomas in the second half of the sixth game against the Minnesota Vikings and would lose his job as the starter at wide receiver.

Thus, Harper started once more in the season finale against the Chicago Bears, in place of an injured Thomas.

On June 10, 1997, the Buccaneers released Alvin before the start of the season.

During his two years in Tampa, he was slowed by injuries and played in only 25 games, where he caught 65 passes for 922 yards and three touchdowns.
